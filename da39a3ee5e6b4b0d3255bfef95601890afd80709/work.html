CommBank Enterprise Mobile App

Retail Banking Network - Shift Management

Decentralisation and automation of shift-work for our casual and part-time frontline branch workforce

Background

Over nine hundred branches across Australia and ten of thousands of frontline workers. Contingent casual and part-time bank specialists across the country wanted the flexibility to be able to work at different locations.

Branch managers wanted the ability to manage their rosters and shift-work remotely with CBA employees from their local network. This digital intervention automates communication, reporting and payments to the respective frontline employee.

Discovery: contextual analysis and interviews

We went out and spoke with frontline staff in Burwood. We interviewed frontline staff, branch managers and regional managers.

Interaction design

We worked on 2 versions of the app for managers and employees, each with unique actions and interactions.

Employee: Onboarding

Onboarding is a 3 step process of profile validation, settings location preferences and availability.

Branch manager: Create a shift

Creating a shift from the perspective of a branch manager.

Employee: Shift acceptance

Employees will be notified when they are offered new shifts. Shift acceptance is based on a first come first serve basis.

Research themes

i. Shift tracking and event notifications

Our branch managers are effective multi-taskers, often triaging customers, supporting their staff with complex loan queries and completing reports. They rarely have the time to manually initiate and broker shifts from potentially available staff members in the area by phone call or text message.

This is why our app kept them informed and up to date as a single shift progresses through several permutations over the course of a single working day â€” our system effectively keeps both the managers and staff in a feedback cycle.

ii. Information Architecture (IA)

We found: As we worked through several iterations of the design. We noticed our IA becoming convoluted and chaotic as a result of designing shift states in isolation of a system. When we thought about the shift states in context of the user, it really came down to two types 'Urgent states: requires attention' or 'Pending/waiting - passive states'.

We learnt: To think deeper about the system and how users may appropriately map consistent visual languages to actions.

iii. Reporting and shift analytics

Shift analytics allows corporate and regional managers to understand resourcing behaviours. It gives actionable insights like, how many shifts are being created, whether shifts are being accepted by frontline staffers and even if managers are excluding staff members from their request.
