NAB Design - Personal Everyday Banking

Digital Expenses Capture

We deconstructed a menacing friction point for many new to bank customers

What's it like to complete a credit application with NAB?
It's a marathon.

Expense capture for NAB and partners
  • Business impact

    Million digital customer interactions every year for new credit products from NAB and partners.

  • Customer impact

    Completing an application is not on the top of the fun list. This experience is located at the last stage of the funnel, already fatigued before they start this task. The only motivating factor is driven by the sunk cost fallacy.

  • Design impact

    We spent over 4 months deconstructing, questioning, prototyping and testing new iterations and challenging assumptions.

Guiding design principles
  • 1. Deliberate when apply or reducing friction

    A flexible system caters for a range digital proficiencies.

  • 2. Discrete experiences create complexities

    The triage points we create should not create paths of no return. (cont'd point 3)

  • 3. Flexibility and utility

    Users should be able to crossover from 1 experience to another without risking an unintentional destructive action.

  • 4. Prioritise visuals over blocks of text

    Visual comprehension is order of magnitude faster than reading and processing text.

  • 5. Develop an IA based on customer mental models

    Understand mental models and patterns to inform hiearchy and taxonomy.

Customer personas (20 customers tested)
  • Persona 1: Financially savvy (20%)

    Manages expenses in a spreadsheet / app. They can distill all their expenses into a single sum.

  • Persona 2: The unsure (20%)

    Financially savvy but takes a little longer to derive a single sum.

  • Persona 3: Needs a little help (60%)

    They can't proceed with certainty without some help.

  • Risky assumptions:

    In the past we assumed all customers needed help with their expenses.

    Bottom line:

    A large portion of customers will need help with calculating their expenses, however if you force 100% of all users down this path, you will certainly aggravate an equally larger portion.

The problem child
  • Problem 1: Up to 40 mandatory inputs

    Each and every customer is asked to complete up to 16 categories of expenses each with 2 mandatory inputs.

  • Problem 2: All categories "had" to be on a single page

    Past research and business assumptions indicates customers wanted to complete their expenses on a single page.

  • Problem 3: Past designs did not account for mental cognition

    If you had 2 seconds to scan this page, what information would you absorb? You wouldn't absorb much at all, given the page is 4000px height with zero-imagery and walls of content.

Opportunities
  • Compartmentalise

    Can we generalise the solution to cater for more mental models?

  • Start a train of thought

    Can we get individuals to think deeply about their expenses?

  • 2 second rule

    You have less than 2 seconds of a customers attention, how do we communicate clearly in that time?

Design v1
First round interventions

Activities: stakeholder engagement, card sorting, design reviews, iterations and prototyping user testing/interviews.

  • Greatly increase scannability

    Replacing text descriptions with light icon visuals. This page immediately became lighter, with increased clarity and cognition.

  • IA: Sort, group and test exercise

    In the past, similar categories were not placed in close vicinity, and as result, a major pain-point was back-tracking and fixing mistakes. We completed card sort exercise before going through our prototype.

  • Learnings

    We tried using examples like 'Netflix' as an example and it was too specific to be effective. Customers thought were literally asking for these expenses instead of seeing them as just examples.

  • Opportunity

    Application fatigued users are the most likely to drop-off at this point.

Second round interventions

Activities: stakeholder engagement, design reviews, iterations, and prototyping user testing/interviews.

  • A. Triage point
  • Express route

    If you know how much you spend each month, I bet you'd rather enter that single figure into an input field and skip the guided path.

  • Equal weighted options

    How do we best present the options side-by-side and communicate the affordance of each journey?

  • B. IA refinements

  • IA groupings and sorting

    Refinements were made to further group categories so that we could improve the way we chunk tasks and provide progression feedback.

  • Interaction model: IA broken into 2 tiers

    Creating 2 tiers allows users to drilldown; challenging that a staged interaction flow is superior to a single page implementation (TBC).

  • C. Mode changes (WIP)

  • Change modes anytimes, even if you're half way through calculating your expenses
