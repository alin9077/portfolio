A central system for IAG's brands and products

This project is still in-flight. IAG is undergoing a digital transformation project that leverages a single system to unite all of IAG brands and products into a central system. This strategic manoeuvre unlocks IAG’s ability to hatch new insurance products at speed, and maintain all polices under a central Customer Relationship Management (CRM) platform for an omni-channel digital and assisted experience.


The first phase of this project is to deliver NRMA’s Buildings and Contents Insurance and Vehicle Insurance products. This program of work delivers a new tech stack and a host of new design and technical constraints. I lead the foundation interaction design and UX frameworks for future and current projects to inherit and leverage. Some of these core frameworks is detailed at a high level in the next sections.

Product qualfiication

A series of questions before the starting the main workflow to determine how the property is occupied.

The property occupancy type allows us to navigate customers to their desired destination, this could be another brand or another product. However, and most importantly – we can alert and stop customers from progressing if they’re disqualified at the very start which saves them time and a frustrating experience.

The exceptions

9 unique types of exceptions can execute all at the same time in the worst-case scenario.

We approached this problem by modelling the edge-cases and working backwards to create a flexible system that priorities customers/business needs. This includes everything from when we hand off to Assisted Channels, when we refer to another IAG product and how we communicate policy exclusions anywhere within the quote funnel.

Policy variations and consent capture

Some customers may be on the boundaries of our policy rules. Variations to policies may also be applied retrospectively in a 24/7 window. This meant we needed a dynamic system to cater for geographic exclusions caused by natural disasters like bushfires, storms, floods, tsunamis and pandemics.

These system events need to be communicated and accepted by the customer. In our approach we wanted to warn customers of exclusion when they provided their locality at the beginning of the funnel. The challenge was that the initial acceptance could be invalidate because another exclusion was applied any time between when they started the quote to paying for the policy.
