Case studies

no.1 - Product qualfication

We studied the path that would be the most disruptive for the customer. It's almost as if everything else hinged on how they answered these questions.

By asking these questions before the app started. We were able to distribute the users into their relevant page / quote funnel. Most importantly, we are able to stop users from proceeding earlier, and shielding them from wasting time trying to buy a product they're not eligible for.

The exceptions (negative paths)

9 unique types of exceptions can execute all at the same time in the worst-case scenario.

We approached this problem by modelling the edge-cases and working backwards to create a flexible system that priorities customers/business needs. This includes everything from when we hand off to Assisted Channels, when we refer to another IAG product and how we communicate policy exclusions anywhere within the quote funnel.


By creating an exception hierarchy, we are able to prioritise and serve up exceptions in a considered manner. This can be observed by how we prioritise clearing input validation before executing service calls, whether we refer to a call centre or a cross-funnel product referals, when geographic exclusions that affect you become active and many more.

Policy variations and consent capture

Some customers may be on the boundaries of our policy rules. Variations to policies may also be applied retrospectively in a 24/7 window. This meant we needed a dynamic system to cater for geographic exclusions caused by natural disasters like bushfires, storms, floods, tsunamis and pandemics.

These system events need to be communicated and accepted by the customer. In our approach we wanted to warn customers of exclusion as soon as they provided us with their address. The challenge was that the initial acceptance could be invalid by the time the customer reaches the final steps of the quote funnel due to the dynamic nature of Australia's natural environment.
