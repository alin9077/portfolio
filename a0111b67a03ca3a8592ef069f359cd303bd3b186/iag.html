NRMA Home and Motor Insurance Pilot

A central system for IAG's brands and products

This project and case-study is still under construction. IAG is undergoing a digital transformation project that leverages a single system to unite all of IAG brands and products into a central system. This strategic manoeuvre unlocks IAG’s ability to hatch new insurance products at speed, and maintain all polices under a central Customer Relationship Management (CRM) platform for an omni-channel digital and assisted experience.

United system to originate and maintain all of IAG's products

My responsibilities The first phase of this project is to deliver NRMA’s Buildings and Contents Insurance and Vehicle Insurance products which I am delivering in unison. This program of work delivers a new technology stack and a host of new design and technical constraints. I am leading and laying the foundational interaction design and UX frameworks for future and current projects to inherit and reuse.

Also responsible for technical designs, UI design and writing content to communicate abstract and complex insurance products and services.

Case studies

no.1 - Product qualfication

Before we start the app, we qualify the customer by asking a series of questions to establish the property occupancy type.

This data point allows customers to navigate to their desired destination, this could be another brand / another product or a referral to speak to someone over the phone. Most importantly – we can alert and stop customers from progressing if they’re disqualified at the very start which saves them time and a frustrating experience.

The exceptions (negative paths)

9 unique types of exceptions can execute all at the same time in the worst-case scenario.

We approached this problem by modelling the edge-cases and working backwards to create a flexible system that priorities customers/business needs. This includes everything from when we hand off to Assisted Channels, when we refer to another IAG product and how we communicate policy exclusions anywhere within the quote funnel.


By creating an exception hierarchy we are able to prioritise and serve up exceptions in a fixed order. This can be observed by how we prioritise clearing input validations and not wasting service calls in a scenario where our data is not clean. Another examples is if we have a better product for customers, we'll re-direct them before we boot them out the funnel. Obvious? Not really. We could only solve all of these exceptions in unison - once we took a step back, analysed and solved this as a system.

Policy variations and consent capture

Some customers may be on the boundaries of our policy rules. Variations to policies may also be applied retrospectively in a 24/7 window. This meant we needed a dynamic system to cater for geographic exclusions caused by natural disasters like bushfires, storms, floods, tsunamis and pandemics.

These system events need to be communicated and accepted by the customer. In our approach we wanted to warn customers of exclusion when they provided their locality at the beginning of the funnel. The challenge was that the initial acceptance could be invalid by the time the customer reaches the final steps of the quote funnel.
